The Creation of Aphrodite

Like most gods and goddesses in Ancient Mythology, the myth of Aphrodite’s creation has many versions and interpretations but I will be explaining the goddess of love’s creation story according to Homer’s, The Illiad. In this story, Aphrodite, the daughter of Zeus and Hera, is portrayed as the main cause of the Trojan War and is also one of Troy’s biggest supporters among the gods and goddesses. Although this is the interpretation that all of The Love Diary’s content is based on, I would like to share another commonly circulated version of Aphrodite’s creation myth. In this alternate version, Aphrodite is the daughter of Uranus and was created when Cronus, Zeus’s father, cut off the genitals of his father, the Greek god of the sky, Ouranos, and threw them into the sea, creating a seafoam in which Aphrodite originated from. These conflicting tales, of Aphrodite’s creation, indicate that her origins are particularly elusive. It seems as though Aphrodite was not native to early Greek religion and her character and mythologies have continued to develop over centuries.
