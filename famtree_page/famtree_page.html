Aphrodite's Family Tree

Character Name Relation to Aphrodite Brief Description
Zeus Father Zeus, considered the god of the sky and the ruler/protector of all gods and humans, was Aphrodite’s father. He is often represented by the symbol of a lightning bolt.
Hera Mother Hera, Aphrodite’s mother in The Illiad, was the queen of the Olympian gods, as well as Zeus’s sister and wife. In most Greek literature, she was worshipped, despite often appearing as the jealous wife of Zeus.
Ares Brother Ares, the god of war, was the Aphrodite’s brother in Ancient Greek Mythology. Although Ares was one of the Olympian deities and the father of many of Aphrodite’s children, according to The Illiad, he did not have a good reputation and even his parents were not fond of him.
Eros Son Eros, the god of love, was one of Aphrodite’s and Ares’s sons. He was also known as the god of fertility.
Harmonia Daughter Harmonia, the goddess of peace, was Aphrodite’s and Ares’s daughter. Ultimately, in most Ancient Greek literature, she metamorphoses into a snake.
Phobos & Deimos Twin Sons Phobos & Deimos, the gods of war and spirits of fear, were Aphrodite’s and Ares’s twin sons. Phobos usually represented panic while his twin brother, Phobos, often conveyed terror or dread.
Aeneas Son Aeneas, the hero of Troy and Rome, was the son of Aphrodite and Anchises, a mortal. He played a crucial role in protecting his city against the Greeks during the Trojan War.
